Description

Noo Saro-Wiwa was brought up in England but she was pulled back every summer to visit her father in Nigeria â€” a place she saw as an irritating alternate universe where she had to give up all her creature comforts and independence. She did not return for several years after her father, the activist Ken Saro-Wiwa, was killed there. She recently decided to come to terms with her father’s land for which he had given his life.

 

Saro-Wiwa flies from the exuberant madness of Lagos to the quiet majesty of the eastern mountains; from the eccentricity of a Nigerian dog show to the Transwonderland Amusement Park’s decrepit kitsch. She discusses Nigerian Christianity, delves into the past of slavery in the region, investigates the corrupting impact of oil, and reflects on Nollywood’s tremendous success.

 

She considers the country as exasperating as ever and still despairs at the injustice and inefficiency that she is facing. Yet she also finds that with its captivating dense tropical rainforest and ancient palaces and temples, it is much more stunning and varied than she’d ever expected. Most engagingly, she introduces us to the many people she encounters and offers us amusing insights into the African woman, her passion, her wit, and her creativity.
